1.8 Why container-fluid

Which scenario below offers a good reason to use the .container-fluid class?

    We'd like to put content in an element that will be sized responsively to a fixed width, and centered within responsive margins, based on the current browser window size.

    We'd like to put content in an element that uses the full width of the browser window at all times, regardless of its size.

    We want to put one container inside of another to achieve a nested effect.

    None of the above are acceptable scenarios for .container-fluid use.

Answer: We'd like to put content in an element that uses the full width of the browser window at all times, regardless of its size.
